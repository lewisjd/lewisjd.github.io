About

As a physics student at the University of Bath, I initially had very little exposure to coding. However, after learning Python in my first year, I became increasingly interested in coding and decided to take a course on C programming to prepare for my second year. I quickly became enamored with coding and realized how useful it was for data analysis, simulations, and interfacing with equipment, such as with the Arduino.

Throughout my coursework, I have worked on several coding projects, including data analysis and implementing numerical methods. One project that I am particularly proud of is creating code to execute the downhill simplex method in multiple dimensions using C programming.

Currently, I am learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to build on my existing coding skills. I recently created this simple website to showcase my coding projects and am working on creating games as a personal project. For example, I have already made a recreation of the 2048 game.

As for my goals and aspirations in coding, I am still exploring where I want to take my skills. My aim is to gain more experience and broaden my knowledge to find where I can best apply my coding skills, whether that be in physics or another field.

In my free time, I enjoy exploring my other hobbies and interests, such as going to the gym and reading. Through my work and collaboration with others in research and projects, I hope to contribute to the broader scientific/tech community and use my skills to help clients or employers achieve their goals.
